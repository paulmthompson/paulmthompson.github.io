Software and Hardware Resources

I make hardware, software, and devices for neuroscience research. I will distribute as much as I am able on this website. You can find categories of these items to the left.

Most of my experiments involve some type of electrophysiology. I use equpment from Intan Technologies, who have made their equipment open source and easily customizable. Their 32+ channel headstages are great, but I use the digital and analog inputs and outputs from their acquisition boards to control my experiments, even if I am not doing high-channel count electrophysiology. I also use their analog inputs as my analog-to-digital convertor for single channel experiments with a stand-alone amplifier. For extracellular electrophysiology, I have created the Intan.jl software package for acquiring data, spike sorting, and controlling experiments. I designed this initially to use with Intan hardware, but it can be adapted to be used with other control systems.

Part of my research studies touch using the whisker system as a model. Consequently, I have had to design experiments that can image the whiskers of awake behaving animals, and process the data. I have made software to control the acquisition from multiple cameras simultaneously, and compress the videos. I have also developed the WhiskerTracking.jl toolbox, a Julia-based analysis package that uses deep learning to track whiskers in high speed videos.
