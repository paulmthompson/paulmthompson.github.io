Software and Hardware Resources

I make hardware, software, and devices for neuroscience research. I will distribute as much as I am able on this website. You can find categories of these items to the left.

For extracellular electrophysiology, I have created the Intan.jl software package for acquiring data, spike sorting, and controlling experiments. I designed this initially to use with Intan hardware, but it can be adapted to be used with other control systems.

Part of my research studies touch using the whisker system as a model. Consequently, I have had to design experiments that can image the whiskers of awake behaving animals, and process the data. I have made software to control the acquisition from multiple cameras simultaneously, and compress the videos. I have also developed the WhiskerTracking.jl toolbox, a Julia-based analysis package that uses deep learning to track whiskers in high speed videos.

Systems neuroscience is highly interdisciplinary, and I often work with peers who have backgrounds in cellular and molecular biology. I am interested in frameworks that let us easily use similar computational and analysis tools without requiring extensive background in linux or programming. I have set up a cluster using Jupyterhub to spawn programs into your web-browser.

Finally, one of the biggest bottle-necks in our lab is our confocal microscope. Several groups have recently shown how one can get near confocal optical quality with significant speed advantages using a single objective light sheet microscope. I have limited optics experience, but have tried to put together a single-objective light sheet of my own.
