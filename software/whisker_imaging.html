Whisker Imaging

Lighting

There are several important features of our light source for whisker imaging: 1) We need the light to be sufficiently intense so that short exposure times can be used to capture the fast moving whiskers; 2) The light source should be collimated to best emphasize the contrast between the whiskers and the background; and 3) The light source should be in the near infrared spectrum so that it is invisible to the animal.

We first need an infrared light source that can provide sufficient intensity. Near infrared (NIR) light emitting diodes (LEDs) are easy to use and cost effective. Remember that LEDs are nonlinear electronic devices which take a roughly fixed voltage, but variable current. The amount of light (and heat) generated by the diode will be proportional to the current input.

There are different options for high-power NIR LEDs on the market. The classic ones referenced in whisker papers from the Svoboda family tree are from Roithner LaserTechnik in Austria. This is a good option for our EU friends. In the USA, there are several options available from large electronic suppliers like Digikey or Mouser.
